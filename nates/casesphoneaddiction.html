Kids Phone Addiction
Behavior Psychology Essay
This solo case study is my response to a company trying to find a way to help kids who are addicted to their phones, ipad or video games. The company was at the research stage and didn’t have a product just yet. I wanted to include this case study in my portfolio because it demonstrates my understanding of behavior psychology and consumer habits. As a deisnger, I take pride in understanding people, understand thier needs and where those needs come from.
Introduction
Download Case PDF
It doesn’t take a genius to know that phones have an addictive side to them but addiction is the wrong word here. We are a very social creature with very specifc needs that must be address in order for us to survive and thrive.
Thinking you can create a product that will “change” the behavior of kids in specific situations isn’t the right approach. Kids are kids and there is a very good reason why they are who they are. In order to illustrate my point, I would like to start with a little trip back in time.
Understanding People
A Tirp Back In Time
6 million years ago, our ancestors seperated from our closest relatives, the Chimpanzees. Modern humans first existed around 300,000 BC in middle Africa. The people of today are still very similllar to our ancestors.
Looking at the daily life, the roles wihin the tribe and the eviroment they lived in can shed a light on the real problem the client face.
As you can see, these roles are very specific and after 300,000 years, those roles are ingranded in us. Young kids, ages 3-14 are loud, energetic and curious, they want to explore the world and try everything. They can speak freely for the first time and likes to get in trouble. Trying to suppress this behaviour is like trying to suppress a dog from barking, it doesn’t work.
Young Males (Age 3-14) · At this age, young males are super active, their learn to hunt small animals, learn to form deep bonds with the other kids, figuring out tribe hierarchy and explore their surroundings.
Infant Males (Age 0-3)  · Play around and learn basic motor skills, speak and being taken care of by the Old Females.
Adult Males (Age 14-40) · Most important male role, at this age, males form the hunting party and defence troops. They get married and have kids, they are the role models for young kids and provide a father role for thier kids.
Old Males (Age 40-Death) · At this age, males are no longer part of the hunting part, they are still great warriors and deal with tribe strategy. They deal with specific tasks and not the daily life of the tribe.
Young Females (Age 3-14) · At this age, young females are super socially active, the fate of the tribe lays in their hands, they are the next generation to give birth and take care of the daily life in the tribe.
Infant Females (Age 0-3) · Play around and learn basic motor skills, speak and being taken care of by the Old Females.
Adult Females (Age 14-40) · Give birth, take care of water, food, fire and clothing. They build tents and take care of the village. They provide the motherly role and teach young girls about the role of females in the tribe.
Old Females (Age 40-Death) · Will not give birth anymore, take care of the tribe “Day Care”. Get involved with tribe strategy and keep all the females of the tribe safe. If there was a case of sexual harassment, rape or physical abusment, the females would turn against that male and if needed the whole tribe, since the tribe can’t survive without females, the tribe would kick out the male who abused the female thus keeping the females safe.
So we figured out that kids are extremely active and social, what would happen if a kid is lonely and needs to be more social? What would happen is that his mind will try to find a way expand energy. Now, if a perent is relactent to provide a social setting, the mind will result to other behaviours like crying or yelling, the mind will test the reaction form the parent, which will be, in most times, some sort of reward in exchange for the kid not crying, like putting TV on or giving the kid a game to play on the phone. Welcome to a Negative Reward System!
A kid can get “addicted” to something because their mind treat the movie or the game as a supplement for social activity. If a parent puts the kid in a stroller when shopping, like a caged animal, of course the kid will cry and yell, why would you stop him from experiencing the world?!
In response for crying the parent will put on a game on the iPad just to make the kid shut up. Of course it works for the short term but in the long term, it takes away from the child ability to experience the social environment, which will eventually lead to poor social skills.
The Reward System, is the system that allows the brain to form habits, patterns and addictions. The parents have a big part in kids addictions.
The Reward System
Social Addiction
Kids experience loneliness just like adults, giving your kids the right social settings is super crucial. Do you want to be lonely? Exactly, why would your kid be any defferent?
Now days, kids dont have the luxury of hanging around kids all day everyday. And this reality, hurt the kids. Our brain needs people, needs a social environment and if the brain is deprived of that, tit will try by any means necessary to achieve that. Phones, TV and games are a supplement for social environment, not an addiction.
Addiction is a very real thing that people experience everyday, from chocolate to TV, from porn to drugs. We need to be very careful with using the word “Addction” because this isnt the right word for this instance. Are we addicted to Oxygen? No, we need it to survive, just like we need water, just like we need food and just like we need social settings.
Kids are not addicted to phones, they are in desperate need for social interactions and social settings. For 6 million years our bloodline lived in a very social settings and only in the last 500 years we changed it. Kids are supplementing social settings with digital products that give the sense of a social setting and of a group setting.
First lets define addiction, Exhibiting a compulsive, need for something.
It just so happen that the people who make digital products are very aware of that and play to this weakness, social media don’t sell “The ability to share your life with the world.” In reality people get addicted to social media because  social media give a snese of group activity, inclusion and a tribe. Which is what our mind crave the most.
Know Your Kids, Know The Damage
Kids are not “addicted” to anything, a kid’s brain is trying to supplement for something. It can be social interactions, it can be sexual pleasure or the feeling of adventure. The key word here is Supplement, this is not real, this is not what the mind really wants, its just a way for the brain to get something it is deprived of. The key player in addiction is not the phone, the tv, drugs or any other physical object.
It’s Loneliness.
People feel lonely, kids feel lonely. Parents can learn a lot about their kids needs from looking at the homeless community. For example, the sole reason most holeless people stay homeless is not lack of money, is not lack of job. In reality people stay homeless because its the only place that have people around them who don’t view them as worthless, they have a positive social setting, friends.
“Put your phone down!” “I’m gonna take your phone away!” and many more stupid things parents say to kids. Parents are ignoring their emotional void. Something that can cause great emotional damage, from anger & mental issues to suicide. There is no case study long enough to explain the damage parents do thier kids by not giving them a social setting.
United State of Kids
Every year more and more kids find themselves spend more and more hours glued to the screen. Sports team have a harder time finding kids that will play, the Army has a harder time recruiting new soldiors because even one if out of shape. The state of kids nowdays are terrible in terms of social settings.  
Although is seems like kids have “friends”, if you would take time to talk to the kids, they will flat out say how lonley they are.
Loneliness is sky rocketing in all ages now days but escapcially with kids.
Wanna solve kids addiction to phones? Start with the parents and the kid’s enviroment.
A New Objective
The was reluctant to stop their “startupy” lifestyle, which is a shame because not everything is an app or software. We had a 3 hour session in which I layed my research and opinion. And… thats was it.  
Every parent want their kid to spend less time in front of the screen right?
Maybe starting to provide a social setting for kids can be a great business opportunity.
I offer the client will research the following opportunities.
Sports School: Open a new school for sports.
After School Activities: Open your own sport class.
Sports Recruitments : Work with parents to get their kids to sport classes.
8 Month Later
8 months after meeting with the client, one of the founders called out of the blue. He wanted to thank me, he was happy to announce that they took my advice seriously and started working on an educational program for parents, a modern day care and a modern school for young kids.  
I was so impressed! I honestly didn’t think they had it in them to go the other way. They showed me their business plan and I must say, I was extremely impressed and proud of their change.
I take pride in understanding user psychology to a level that guides my desicion making, I wanted to display my ability to take the right choice instead of falling to simple ideas that are flawed by nature. I may not be the greatest designer but I’m pretty competent at addressing the psychological side of things, getting to the key pain points and figuring out what approach can be taken.
