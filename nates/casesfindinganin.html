Finding an “In”
Research Case
The is my solo research case on how I researched a way to get into the restaurant software market for Rill Digital, a small software company. In this case study I try to detail my research process and the key points in my research method.
The research had come from a road mapping session the board had done. I was the only user researcher in the team and was tasked research already exisiting products, find weakness and strengths in them while trying to understand how a small company like Rill Digital can get involved with this market.

The Research Methodology
For this project, I decided on two different methods to fit the timeline, Interviews and surveys. I decided to conduct 30 mins long interviews with users who order food online on a regular basis. For surveys I used Survey Monkey, because everyone order food online, finding users wasn’t a problem.
In-Person Interviews & Online Surevy.
Overall, I held 32 of these interviews
The first 10 minutes of each interview focused on their day-to-day and how ordering food online fits into their days. We also discussed competitors and other products. The latter 20 minutes was spent doing a complete walkthrough of a product they use. I observed them using the app and then asked clarifying questions whenever necessary. I chose this method because it allowed me to understand people’s mental models of online food delivery and see how people interacted with the app. With this interaction, I could pinpoint the top pain points.
Since the team would use this information for a future roadmap, I wanted to make sure the findings were as reliable and valid as possible. In this case, I used the insights we found of the top pain points and surveyed over 100 users on each pain point's importance and current satisfaction.
The Research Goals
I wanted to better understand how users think about ordering food online, using apps and how they interact with them.
What were the goals of the research project? Discover user’s mental models on how and why they order food online. Uncover the different products people are using to order food online, and their experience with these products. Evaluate how people are using the available products on the market & their top pain points.
My way of asking questions can be seperated into two categories, Probe and Open. When I ask a “Probe” question, I usually looking for a single word answer, I try to get raw to gadge the how ideal the user is. On the other hand, why I ask an “Open” question, I don’t look for data, I look for emotions, feeling and body language. When asking open ended question I want to “play” association games with the person, I want to get the feeling behind his answer rather than the answer itself.
Some of the questions I asked:
[Probe] How often do you check a new restaurant?
[Probe] How often do you prefer ordering over sitting in a restaurant?
[Probe] How often do you order food?
[Probe] How often do you eat in restaurants
[Open] What did you like the most about using this product?
[Open] What did you like the least?
[Open] What, if anything, surprised you about the experience?
[Open] What, if anything, caused you frustration?
User Recruitment
For this research, I was looking for a mixture of users and non-users. I did this because I wanted to make sure I got a fresh perspective on the products and see how first-time users respond to the experience of ordering food online and checking out restaurants. This helped me ensure I wasn’t just looking at power users that were “used to” existing products. Since every person eats twice a day, finding users wasn’t a big deal. I wanted to set requirement for potential users, I based my craterias on market data and consumer data.
Tools for recruitment I used varied from asking strangers on the street to visiting my friends companies and using SurveyMoneky, TypeForm, Maze & Google Forms.

Usability Tests
During the interview sessions, I used very open-ended questions. To ensure this was the case, I used the TEDW approach to frame most of my questions. This led to an extremely open conversation, which allowed us to understand mental models and pain points without leading them. I feel that open ended question, visual question work best when interviewing people, people like to talk, specially about themselves, so visual question works best for me.
When it came to the walkthrough portion of the interview, I had users walk me through the last order they made (whether it was on Wolt, Google Maps etc…) and then prompted the participants to talk through each step in detail.

Analysis & Synthesis
I synthesize research sessions by listening to each session 24 hours afterward and transcribing the session directly into an excel spreadsheet. I then label the data with common tags (such as pain points, needs, goals, tools). I also used this opportunity to highlight relevant quotes.
After a few interviews, I held a mini synthesis session with the board. Every stakeholder took three interviews, listened to them, and tagged the data. In the session, we mapped most of the trends. Since we were hyper-focused on pain points for this study, we looked into those and goals, needs, and other products participants were using.
The reason I did this mini-synthesis session is to get the board involved and figure out if this is something they want to put effort in.
After synthesizing each session, I put together a report for the session. I called this a Research Snapshot as it gives a high-level overview of what happened in that particular session. It is beneficial for teams to have this information at-a-glance because they ingest it more easily and take action.

Impact
This research helped the me understand the customers pain points and the experiences they encounter. Regardless of if the comapny will go into this market or not, I built a nice solid database from which we can extract information in the future. During the quarterly planning, the insights and the personas were used in the prioritization and planning process, the pain points were highlighted and are being tackled through ideation, prototyping, and more research. At a larger level, the marketing team was able to improve their content strategy, including user aqusition strategy and marketing campaigns strategy.
The comapny approach to the product moved from a “software” based product to truly help the restaurants and users a like. Which means, moving from B2B products to a more B2C approach.
Many pain points came out of the research. I am now following-up on these pain points with ideation and usability testing, because of the pain points I found, the board instructed me to sketch a simple working product that will answer all of the pain points and test it.
More than that, I was now tasked with understanding the differrent products in the market and to understand if a digital product is really the right answer. I will continue to iterate on the personas as we learn more. I also will validate the customer journey map with more interviews.
Reflections
People actually used the research!
The methodology allowed me to address all of the goals and pain points. Stakeholders participated in the research. The mindset of the company shifted towards a more user-centricity approach.
Recruitment took a very long time, I will target better next time on be more focused recruitment criteria. It took a while to get the real pain points out of users, I will learn more on the value of research.
People want the to order food from many places but tend to have a few favorites.
People like to order food for budgetary and convenience reasons.
People care about tracking their food in order to time it with something.
People order food because something went wrong with their food and people know what they want before even opning up the product.
All and all, I think I was a pretty good research given the scope and approach. I hope to learn more and about product and user reseach, to grow as a researcher and to understand people better
